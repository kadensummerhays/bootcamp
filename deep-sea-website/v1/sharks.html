Sharks

  • Great White Shark
  • Tiger Shark
  • Lemon Shark
  • Lantern Shark
  • White Tipped Reef Shark

One sad fact: Great Whites are notorious for attacking us. The reason for this is not because they hate us, but really, they think we are their food. Who can blame them for that?

Food: Great White Sharks mainly attack walruses, sea lions and sometimes penguins. They also attack schools of tuna.

Looks: The reason they are called "white" because their stomachs are white. They are "great", because they are massive and very strong.

Mouth: They have very strong mouths. Their top teeth are not serrated (sawlike), however, their lower teeth are. The purpose for their top teeth are to hold onto their prey while their lower teeth shed their prey in pieces.

Food: Tiger sharks have strong teeth, because they hunt reef animals, like turtles, crabs and groopers. Some tiger sharks hunt in the reefs.

Looks: The reason they are called a "tiger" shark, is because they have stripes on their back, just like a tiger has.

Personality: Another attribute they have is their aggresiveness. They are much more aggressive than the great white shark.

Food: The lemon shark is very fast, because he has a big tail which allows him to push through the water quickly. This allows him to catch his dinner in a way that other sharks are unable to.

Their prey: Lemon sharks hunt all kinds of fish. They eat sardines, red snappers, groopers and really big fish, along with schools of tuna.

Looks: They are called the "lemon" shark, because they have yellow on their back. They look like a bull shark, because they have a pointy nostril like a bull shark. However, they don't share any other features that the bull shark has.

Sense of Heat: Lemon sharks not only have smell to hunt for their prey like other sharks, but they also can sense other's animal's heat, therefore allowing them to find their prey better. This isn't the only shark that can do this.

Looks: Lantern sharks get their name because their stomach and their fins glow like a lantern. They have very small eyes, but can see in the dark. They aren't very big sharks. In fact, they are about as big as an adult's palm.

Food: They hunt very small fish. For example, fish like small star fish and others that live in the midnight zone; a place where strange and unsual fish live.

Ways to get their prey: The lantern shark swims around their prey until they get the fish's attention. The prey will get its attention from its glow and that is when it attacks!

Looks: The white tipped reef shark has a white tip on all their fins, hence the name "white tipped". Their tail is just like a lemon shark, which allows them to hunt sardines as easy as lemon sharks do.

Food: White tipped reef sharks use heat and smell to find their prey, just like a lemon shark. They like to eat angel fish, frog fish, clown fish, groopers, and red snappers.

Friends: This shark has a friend! Their friend is called a pilot fish. They clean the sharks teeth so they don't get cavities.
